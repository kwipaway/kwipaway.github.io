The Fascinating Furcula: A Link Between Birds and Dinosaurs

Introduction

The furcula, commonly known as the wishbone, is a forked bone found in most birds and some extinct dinosaurs. Let's explore its fascinating features:

Structure and Function

  1. Structure and Function

    • The furcula is formed by the fusion of the two clavicles.
    • In birds, its primary function is to strengthen the thoracic skeleton to withstand the rigors of flight.
    • It acts as a strut between a bird's shoulders, connecting to each of the bird's scapulae (shoulder blades).
    • Together with the coracoid and scapula, it forms a unique structure called the triosseal canal, which houses a strong tendon connecting the supracoracoideus muscles to the humerus.
    • During flight, the furcula plays a crucial role in lifting the wings during the recovery stroke.
    • As the thorax compresses during downstroke, the furcula's upper ends spread apart and then contract, acting like a spring in the pectoral girdle.
    • Some bird species lack a furcula, while others have vestigial or oversized versions¹².

  2. Dinosaur Connection

    • Several groups of theropod dinosaurs have also been found with furculae.
    • These include dromaeosaurids, oviraptorids, tyrannosaurids, troodontids, coelophysids, and allosauroids.
    • The furcula's presence in dinosaurs sheds light on their evolutionary history and adaptations³.

  3. Folklore

    • The wishbone has inspired superstitions and divination practices.
    • Traditionally, people would break a goose's wishbone, believing that the longer piece would grant a wish.
    • This tradition dates back to at least the Late Medieval Period¹.

So next time you enjoy a chicken dinner, remember that the wishbone you might pull apart has ancient ties to both birds and dinosaurs!
